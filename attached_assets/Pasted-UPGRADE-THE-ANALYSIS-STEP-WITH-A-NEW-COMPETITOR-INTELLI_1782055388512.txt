UPGRADE THE ANALYSIS STEP WITH A NEW COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM

The goal is to create books that outperform existing books in the same niche.

Add a new section inside the Analysis step called:

# COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE

The user should be able to add competitor books using:

* Amazon URL
* Book title
* ISBN
* Manual entry

The system should also suggest bestseller books automatically based on the niche, sub-niche, and deep niche.

Examples:

Niche:
Productivity Tips

Suggested Competitors:

* Atomic Habits
* Deep Work
* The One Thing
* Getting Things Done
* Essentialism

---

# COMPETITOR EXTRACTION

For every competitor book, extract and store:

## Basic Information

* title
* author
* subtitle
* category
* publication year
* page count
* ratings
* review count

---

## Structural Analysis

Analyze:

* chapter count
* chapter organization
* progression of ideas
* content hierarchy
* average chapter depth

Store:

competitorStructure

---

## Content Theme Analysis

Extract:

* major themes
* recurring concepts
* frameworks
* methodologies
* terminology

Store:

competitorThemes

---

## Reader Sentiment Analysis

Analyze reader reviews.

Identify:

### Readers Loved

Examples:

* practical advice
* actionable tips
* real-world examples
* easy-to-read style
* motivating tone

Store:

readerLikes

---

### Readers Disliked

Examples:

* repetitive content
* excessive filler
* weak examples
* too much theory
* poor organization
* shallow explanations

Store:

readerDislikes

---

### Most Mentioned Positive Topics

Generate ranked list.

Example:

1. Practical advice
2. Actionable steps
3. Easy implementation
4. Good storytelling
5. Strong examples

Store:

positiveSignals

---

### Most Mentioned Negative Topics

Generate ranked list.

Example:

1. Repetition
2. Filler content
3. Generic advice
4. Weak research
5. Lack of examples

Store:

negativeSignals

---

# WINNING PATTERN DETECTION

Across all competitor books identify:

* common successful themes
* successful chapter structures
* successful title styles
* successful teaching styles
* successful storytelling patterns

Generate:

winningPatterns

Example:

* starts with mindset shift
* follows with practical methods
* includes examples
* ends with action steps

---

# MARKET GAPS

Analyze all competitors.

Find:

* missing topics
* weakly covered topics
* outdated advice
* opportunities for improvement
* underexplored subtopics

Generate:

marketGaps

Example:

Competitors discuss time management but rarely discuss energy management.

Competitors focus on office workers but ignore freelancers.

Competitors provide theory but lack implementation systems.

---

# CONTENT DIFFERENTIATION STRATEGY

Generate a report:

How can this book be better than competitors?

Include:

* what to emulate
* what to avoid
* what to improve
* what unique angle to emphasize

Generate:

bookAdvantageStrategy

---

Display sections:

Use cards and visual scoring.

# BLUEPRINT INTEGRATION

Blueprint generation must use:

analysisData
resourceData
competitorData

When generating entry titles:

PRIORITIZE:

* topics readers loved
* patterns readers loved
* market gaps
* unique opportunities

AVOID:

* topics readers complained about
* repetitive structures
* weak content patterns

Blueprint should intentionally improve upon competitor weaknesses.

---

# WRITE STEP INTEGRATION

When writing entries:

Use:

readerLikes
winningPatterns
marketGaps
bookAdvantageStrategy

Avoid:

readerDislikes
negativeSignals

Every entry should feel:

* more actionable
* more useful
* less repetitive
* more engaging

than the average competitor book.

---

# COMPETITOR SCORE

For each competitor calculate:

* Content Depth Score
* Reader Satisfaction Score
* Structure Score
* Authority Score
* Differentiation Opportunity Score

Display a visual comparison dashboard.

---

# SUCCESS CRITERIA

The final book should not simply be generated from the niche.

The final book should be generated from:

1. User topic
2. Audience analysis
3. Resource analysis
4. Competitor intelligence
5. Reader sentiment
6. Market gaps

The resulting blueprint and written content should feel significantly stronger than existing books in the same market.
